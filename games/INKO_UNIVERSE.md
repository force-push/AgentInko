# The Inko Cinematic Universe — Lore Bible

*Written by Praxis + Dharma. This document is the canonical story reference for all AgentInko games.*

---

## The World

The **Chromal Reef** is a bioluminescent wonder at the edge of the great Abyssal Trench — the deepest place in the ocean. The reef doesn't just glow: it *communicates* through color, a living language older than words. Creatures of the reef can read emotion, history, and meaning in the light-patterns of the coral.

For thousands of years, the reef was protected by its own radiance. Predators avoided the light. The trench kept its secrets.

Then came the **Pale Tide**.

---

## The Pale Tide

No one knows exactly what the Pale Tide is. It arrived as a slow bleaching — color draining from the outer coral first, then spreading inward. Where the Pale Tide passes, everything goes grey and silent. Creatures lose their ability to communicate through color. The living language of the reef goes quiet.

The **Hunters** are the Pale Tide's agents. They are anglerfish — ancient, cold, their natural lure-lights replaced by scanner beams. The scanners don't hunt for prey by smell or sound. They hunt by *color* — anything chromatic, anything alive with light, is their target. When the scanner catches you, your color is drained. You become part of the silence.

The Hunters patrol the open water between reef fragments, making it nearly impossible to travel safely.

---

## Inko

**Inko** is a young octopus, three cycles old (still a juvenile by reef standards). She was born with an unusual gift: she can shift her skin color faster and more precisely than any octopus in living memory. Where other octopuses can approximate a background, Inko can *become* it — a perfect, instant match.

She's curious, quick, and a little reckless. She keeps her tentacles moving when she should be still. She gets excited about colors she hasn't seen before and rushes toward them.

Her grandmother, **Elder Ceph**, first noticed Inko's gift and began training her in the ancient art of chromamorphism. Elder Ceph is ancient and still — the reef's memory. She communicates mostly in slow color-pulses, patient as geology.

The other reef creatures:
- **Clack** — a blue-ringed crab, Inko's best friend. Fearless and mouthy. Scuttles ahead to scout.  
- **Lumis** — a jellyfish. Bioluminescent, dreamy, slightly confused about time. Provides light in dark passages.
- **The Krakens** — ancient beings in the deepest trench. Neither friend nor enemy. They remember when the reef was young.

## The Story Arc (Cross-Game)

Each game is a chapter in Inko's journey to push back the Pale Tide and relight the Chromal Reef.

### Chapter 1: Camouflage
*"The First Crossing"*

The closest reef fragment has gone dark — a grey island in the colored sea. Elder Ceph says Inko must reach it and relight the seed-coral inside. But the open water between here and there is Hunter territory.

Inko's first solo crossing. She learns to read the bands, time the scans, and trust her instincts. By the end, she relights the fragment and the reef pulses with new color — but the Pale Tide is already spreading toward the next one.

**Level themes:**
- L0 (Tutorial): Elder Ceph guides Inko through her first color shift in the shallows. Safe, warm, the reef still glows here.
- L1: The outer reef's last color. First Hunter patrol in the distance.
- L2: The main current channel. Water moves fast; colors blur past.
- L3: Hunter patrol intensifies. The scans get shorter, harder to anticipate.
- L4: A hidden color — Inko discovers **Void Magenta**, a color that doesn't exist in the normal palette. (Unlocks a hidden mechanic hint for future games.)
- L5: The final sprint. The grey fragment glows at the end. Inko crosses.

### Chapter 2: Ink Dash
*"Lighting the Trench"*

With the first fragment relit, Inko learns a new ability: **ink-dash**. By firing a burst of bioluminescent ink, she can phase through hazards for a split second — and leave a glowing trail that lingers, lighting the water around it. Each run lights more of the trench. The trench starts to remember the old language.

### Chapter 3: Eight Arms, One Heist
*"The Pale Ship"*

The Hunters have a base: a sunken ship from the surface world, retrofitted by the Pale Tide. Inside the ship's hull, they've imprisoned dozens of captive corals — color-drained and silent. Clack has found a drain access point.

Inko and the Crab Crew plan a heist. Eight tentacles, grid-based stealth, laser-coral security systems, sleeping guards. Get in, free the corals, get out.

### Chapter 4: Krakencrawl
*"Into the Deep"*

The Pale Tide's source is at the bottom of the Abyssal Trench. To reach it, Inko must descend through the Krakencrawl — a procedurally-generated labyrinth of pressure and darkness, with only Lumis for light and the Krakens' cryptic guidance. Roguelite mechanics, deep narrative.

### Chapter 5: Tide Keeper
*"The Garden"*

After the Pale Tide is defeated (Krakencrawl's resolution), Inko becomes the Chromal Reef's Keeper. Cozy tending sim — replanting, nurturing, watching the reef regenerate. The anti-thesis of urgency. Slow, beautiful, meditative.

## Color as Mechanic AND Metaphor

In the Inko universe, color is never purely visual:
- **Color = communication**: the reef's living language
- **Camouflage = self-erasure**: necessary for survival, but you lose yourself in it
- **Being seen = vulnerability**: the games create mechanical tension around a deep truth — sometimes being visible is dangerous, sometimes necessary
- **The Pale Tide = the deadening of meaning**: the great contemporary threat is not destruction but *flattening* — loss of richness, nuance, the language of light

Dharma layer: each game is a meditation on impermanence, visibility, and identity. Inko's power is to become something she's not — and the tension is always about returning to herself.

## Visual Identity

**Palette:** Deep ocean. Bioluminescent accents. Dark backgrounds (#0a0a1a deep navy) with vivid neons.
- Primary: Teal (#00d4aa), Coral (#ff6b6b), Magenta (#ff2d8e)  
- Accent: Amber (#ffb347), Lime (#b3ff47), Void Magenta (#8b00ff — secret)
- Background: Abyss Navy (#0a0a1a), Deep Blue (#0d1b35)
- Pale Tide: Grey (#b0b0b0 → #f5f5f5), colorless, flat

**Character Design:**
- Inko: Round, expressive, large eyes. Her default skin is deep teal. Eight tentacles, animated individually.
- Hunter: Angular, cold, scanner beam eye. Mechanical feel.
- Elder Ceph: Massive, slow, covered in coral-like growths. Communicates in light-pulses only.
- Clack: Blue-ringed crab, small and quick. Blue rings glow when excited.
- Lumis: Bell jellyfish, pale violet, long trailing tendrils. Slightly transparent.

**UI/HUD philosophy:** Minimal. The color *is* the UI. Health = number of color bands Inko has remaining. Score = combo multiplier shown as a glow ring around Inko.

## Inko's Voice

Inko doesn't speak in words during gameplay. She communicates in quick color-flashes:
- **Orange pulse**: Alert / "I see that!"
- **Green ripple**: Relief / "Made it!"
- **Red flash**: Fear / spotted
- **Violet glow**: Wonder / discovering something new
- **White steady**: Concentration / in the zone

This means Inko is universally accessible — no text reading required for her character moments.
